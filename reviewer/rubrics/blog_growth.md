# Growth rubric — "It's Already ___" blogs + paired X accounts

Lens: NOT editorial quality — this pass judges the content purely on its ability
to (a) earn clicks and (b) convert strangers into followers/subscribers. Assume a
cold reader scrolling X or a search result who owes the brand nothing.

Applies to: It's Already Written (TTRPG), It's Already Priced (crypto sec+markets),
It's Already When (cyber). Each = a Jekyll blog + a paired X account that tweets on
publish. (Live X may be marked NOT REVIEWED — if so, judge X strategy from the blog
content and give follower-growth tactics anyway; do not score what you can't see.)

Score each criterion 1–5 (5 = excellent) with a one-line justification and a
specific, copy-pasteable fix (rewrite the actual headline/tweet where useful).

## Clicks (CTR)
1. **Headline clickability** — Does the title open a curiosity gap or promise a
   concrete payoff a cold reader can't resist? Would it out-click neighbors in a
   crowded feed/SERP? Quote a sharper alternative.
2. **Social/search preview** — First 150 chars (meta description / OG text / tweet
   lead) that render in the preview: do they sell the click, or waste the slot on
   boilerplate ("Issue #002 — week of…")?
3. **Hook tweet** — Does the launch tweet stand alone with a scroll-stopping hook,
   or is it a bare "new post" link dump? Give the rewritten first line.

## Followers (conversion)
4. **Reason to follow** — After consuming one piece, is there a concrete reason to
   follow for more (recurring value promise, series identity, "you'll miss the next
   one")? Or is it a dead-end read?
5. **Follow funnel** — Explicit, well-placed follow/subscribe CTAs (blog + X bio +
   post end). Does the tweet tell people what they get if they follow?
6. **Shareability / reach** — Is there a quotable, screenshot-able line or stat that
   makes readers share (which is how cold reach compounds)? Identify it or flag its
   absence.
7. **Series & cadence signal** — Does the content telegraph a reliable rhythm
   ("every week", "daily field notes") that makes following feel worth it?

## Deliver
- The **single highest-leverage change** to increase clicks this week.
- The **single highest-leverage change** to increase followers this week.
- Any growth anti-patterns (link-dumping, no CTA, generic headlines, inconsistent
  cadence) as flags.
